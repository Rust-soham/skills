### Core Claims

`SOURCE`: A compounding curriculum is a dependency graph, not a topic list. The
steps are boundaries around transformations that make later transformations
easier ([988-1003](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L988)).

`SOURCE`: Three mechanisms are proposed:

1. **Skill compounding**: a later capability increases the value of prior
   capabilities.
2. **Threshold concepts**: some concepts reorganize perception and become hard
   to unsee.
3. **Transfer-appropriate processing**: learning conditions should resemble use
   conditions.

These are combined at
[988-996](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L988).

`SOURCE`: Domain content is context-dependent. The reusable part is the process
for discovering high-leverage thresholds and prerequisite models
([1095-1104](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1095)).

`SOURCE`: The learner should develop generative perception: seeing structure,
forming new questions, deriving implications, and knowing which unexplored
angle to test next
([1031-1038](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1031),
[711-715](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L711)).

### Compounding Threshold Ladder

#### 0. Domain Autopsy

Determine:

- What experts perceive that beginners do not
- Which concepts cause broad perceptual shifts
- Which missing prerequisite models create invisible ceilings

Use expert retrospectives about changed thinking, not only tutorials about
content
([1004-1012](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1004)).

#### 1. Substrate Models

Identify the domain's lowest accessible ground truth. Make these models
automatic enough that higher-order reasoning does not spend working memory
reconstructing basics
([1016-1025](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1016)).

#### 2. Early Perceptual Shift

Target an early threshold that changes what questions the learner generates.
The domain example moves from normal operation to failure modes. The broader
mechanism is a change in the unit of perception
([1029-1038](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1029)).

#### 3. Use Conditions

Practice in conditions that preserve the important pressures, uncertainty, and
resistance of real use. Fail early and constrain practice so feedback is
information-dense
([1042-1050](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1042)).

#### 4. Edge Practice

Calibrate difficulty to produce meaningful failure without overload. The source
proposes a 30-50% failure range and immediate feedback
([1054-1058](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1054)).

#### 5. Recontextualization

After a threshold, revisit prior knowledge through the new lens. This is
described as compression rather than revision: many earlier facts collapse into
fewer generative ideas
([1062-1068](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1062)).

#### 6. Teaching

Explain or publish before feeling fully ready. Explanation exposes missing
links, forces abstraction, and reveals the next threshold
([1072-1076](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1072)).

### Threshold Definition

The source adopts five properties:

- Transformative
- Troublesome
- Irreversible
- Integrative
- Bounded

([1430-1440](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1430))

It then sharpens the learner's formulation into four mental operations:

- Reframe the primitives
- Identify invariants
- Rewire relations
- Change the lens

([1354-1373](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1354))

Thresholds may be gradual and sequential. A later threshold may not be visible
until an earlier one is crossed
([1375-1387](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1375)).

### Threshold Engineering Procedure

1. **Find bottlenecks, not content.** Locate recurring plateaus and expert mental
   moves beginners cannot perform
   ([1448-1458](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1448)).
2. **Expose the troublesome zone.** Begin with the plausible old intuition and
   create dissonance by showing where it fails. Do not erase the necessary
   struggle
   ([1462-1475](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1462)).
3. **Create an integration point.** Present varied cases, then ask what remains
   invariant across them
   ([1479-1493](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1479)).
4. **Support liminality.** Recur through multiple representations and repeated
   failures at increasing abstraction
   ([1497-1507](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1497)).
5. **Make the shift irreversible.** Apply it quickly to a real challenge,
   explain it, and create public or durable output
   ([1511-1521](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1511)).
6. **Sequence thresholds as gates.** Each transformation should enable the next
   ([1525-1535](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1525)).

Compressed principle: make the counter-intuitive unavoidable, the integration
visible, and the application immediate
([1539-1543](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1539)).

### Signals

Evidence of a real threshold:

- A recurring beginner mistake disappears
- Separate ideas become connected
- The old model feels inefficient
- The learner explains more with fewer ideas

([1389-1398](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1389))

The troublesome feeling is diagnostic only within bounds: easy may mean the
threshold was missed; impossible may mean prerequisites are missing
([1539-1543](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L1539)).

### Failure Modes

- Topic inventories mistaken for curriculum
- Advanced content without substrate models
- Abstract study that cannot be retrieved under real conditions
- Practice below the learner's edge
- Difficulty above the learner's current prerequisites
- Revising facts without recontextualizing prior models
- Explaining the answer before the old model visibly fails

### Refinements and Tensions

`REFINEMENT`: The domain-specific sequence first frames compounding as each
technical layer feeding later layers
([773-894](../../../transcripts/Actual%20roadmap%20to%20become%20a%20elite%20hacker%20like%20the%20o.md#L773)).
The later abstraction replaces those topics with threshold gates and cognitive
dependencies.

`QUESTION`: "Build the lowest-level mental models first" and "target the first
threshold early even if it violates normal curriculum order" are compatible
only if prerequisite sufficiency is diagnosed rather than assumed.

`QUESTION`: "Do not smooth out difficulty" must later be reconciled with the
older transcripts' goal of removing unwanted friction. The likely distinction
is productive model-breaking friction versus incidental retrieval and tooling
friction, but that is not established from S01 alone.

`QUESTION`: The source uses a numeric 30-50% failure target. Treat this as a
provisional calibration heuristic, not yet a system invariant.

`QUESTION`: The "failure mode shift" is powerful in adversarial and engineering
domains, but may be one instance of a broader first threshold rather than a
universal first threshold.

### Candidate Implications

`SYNTHESIS`: A future system may need separate operations for:

- Discovering a domain's threshold graph
- Diagnosing the learner's current node
- Designing a model-breaking experience
- Verifying a generative threshold crossing
- Recontextualizing prior knowledge

These are implications, not yet candidate skills.
