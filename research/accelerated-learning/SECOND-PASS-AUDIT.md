# Second-Pass Omission Audit

## Question

Does any learning mechanism in `transcripts/` remain absent from the research
archive after the initial audit and the nine-file transcript restoration?

## Method

The second pass used five checks:

1. Confirmed one source note for each of the 16 transcripts.
2. Re-ran the 91-range, 18,205-line coverage inventory with zero gaps or
   overlaps.
3. Reprocessed the nine restored transcripts and updated every affected line
   citation.
4. Compared transcript structural markers, learner-authored corrections,
   domain examples, product branches, and technical demonstrations against all
   source notes.
5. Searched repeated mechanisms across transcripts against the concept index,
   synthesis, architecture, state contracts, tensions, and operational rules.
6. Distinguished true absence from a mechanism retained in a source note but
   not promoted into the reusable concept layer.

## Result

No transcript region or source-level mechanism remains absent. The restored
corpus is fully partitioned and the source notes now preserve contextual
branches rather than retaining only their generalized conclusions.

The pass did find three recurring mechanisms that were present in source notes
but underrepresented in the canonical concept layer:

1. **Directed confusion and feedback density**: compress the slow model-building
   phase through correct focus, clear evidence, short causal loops, and early
   misalignment detection.
2. **Bifocal learner and domain modeling**: reason simultaneously about the
   learner's evidenced representation and the expert dependency or threshold
   graph.
3. **Metacognitive probe internalization**: move from agent-generated questions
   to learner-generated diagnostic questions and correction of the learning
   process itself.

These are now concepts
[20](concepts/20-directed-confusion-and-feedback-density.md),
[21](concepts/21-bifocal-learner-and-domain-modeling.md), and
[22](concepts/22-metacognitive-probe-internalization.md).

The restoration pass added four refinements to existing concepts:

1. **Threshold mining:** compare varied cases, rotate representations, and find
   the invariant that survives before treating a threshold as a grounded node.
2. **First-move ownership:** AI may frame the diagnostic situation, but the
   learner should make the first hypothesis, prediction, interpretation, or
   attempted reconstruction when safe.
3. **Compounding versus resetting loops:** faster interaction compounds only
   when failure becomes connected, reusable structure.
4. **Cognitive ergonomics:** AI acts as the environment around learner-owned
   decisions by reducing retrieval and structure-reconstruction overhead.

## Buried Refinements Promoted

The pass also made four source-level nuances explicit in existing cards:

- Externalize and name models; retain superseded models and failed boundaries.
- Silence is a valid scaffold while the learner is producing useful evidence.
- Plateaus and near-misses can be liminal evidence before a threshold crossing.
- Intervention cadence should follow attention and error quality, not a fixed
  session rhythm.

## Remaining Limits

- Domain-specific technical claims remain contextual unless independently
  verified.
- Unsupported scientific, coercive, and identity-manipulation claims remain
  visible as rejected material, not learning mechanisms.
- Complete semantic extraction does not imply that every repeated phrase
  deserves a separate concept or runtime instruction.
- Time-sensitive cybersecurity, software, and labor-market claims remain
  contextual until independently verified.
