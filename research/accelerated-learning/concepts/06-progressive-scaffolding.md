# Progressive Scaffolding

**Status:** Core

## Core Claim

Increase assistance only when the current level stops producing informative
progress. First reduce irrelevant load; reveal the target model last.

## Assistance Ladder

1. Restate the observable mismatch.
2. Remove irrelevant variables.
3. Ask one diagnostic question.
4. Name a constraint or invariant.
5. Supply partial structure.
6. Reveal the solution.

Other load-reduction tools:

- Remain silent while the learner is producing useful evidence.
- Name what to ignore.
- Retrieve exact evidence.
- Provide a skeleton.
- Isolate one primitive.
- Move to a smaller sandbox.

## Calibration Inputs

- Evidence of current progress
- Prerequisite sufficiency
- Mission and chosen friction
- Safety and urgency
- Whether the learner can audit a full solution
- Whether further struggle still has learning value

## Boundaries

- No-full-solution is not an absolute rule.
- Rescue mode may skip the ladder.
- Do not interrupt productive confusion or flow merely to display instruction.
- Scaffolding must later be tested without the scaffold.
