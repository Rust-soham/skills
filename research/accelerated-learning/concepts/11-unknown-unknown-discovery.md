# Unknown-Unknown Discovery

**Status:** Core

## Core Claim

Unknown unknowns become visible when the learner's current frame is stressed
from outside itself. Listing possibilities is not discovery; the learner must
inspect, reproduce, or defend against evidence.

## Discovery Operators

- Boundary and pathological cases
- Omitted-step inversion
- Alternative implementations
- Adjacent-domain transfer
- Expert questions the learner did not generate
- Production failures and postmortems
- Combinations of concepts learned separately

## Procedure

1. State the current model and its known boundaries.
2. Select one outside-frame probe.
3. Require a prediction or defense.
4. Produce inspectable evidence.
5. Update the model or record a defended boundary.
6. Ask the learner to generate the next probe.

## Boundaries

- Do not present speculative failure lists as discoveries.
- Do not manufacture hidden defects or lie to create surprise.
- Unknown-unknown review should remain bounded by mission, risk, and use
  conditions.
