# Compression and Pattern Extraction

**Status:** Core

## Core Claim

After a model repair, compress the experience into a reusable invariant,
boundary, and failure pattern. This makes later reasoning faster without
discarding causal structure.

## Extract

- Failed assumption
- Evidence that broke it
- Invariant that survived variation
- Boundary where the old model remains useful
- Compressed rule
- Abstract failure pattern
- Other contexts where the pattern may recur

## Why It Compounds

Compression reduces working-memory cost, turns isolated incidents into
retrievable structures, and creates anchors for later analogies. The useful
compression preserves what predicts and explains, not merely a slogan.

## Verification

- Apply the rule to a changed case.
- Detect an old-pattern failure before execution.
- Reconstruct the reasoning from the compressed rule.
- Transfer the invariant to another domain and state where it breaks.

## Boundaries

- Do not compress before causal diagnosis.
- Do not promote a project incident globally without transfer evidence.
- Preserve superseded models and their useful boundaries.
