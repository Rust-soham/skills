# Metacognitive Probe Internalization

**Status:** Core

## Core Claim

The terminal capability is not only answering a good probe. It is independently
noticing uncertainty, generating the probe that would discriminate between
models, and correcting the learning process that produced the gap.

## Two Diagnostic Targets

- **Object level:** Which subject-matter model is missing, vague, or wrong?
- **Meta level:** Which reasoning, framing, attention, or help-seeking pattern
  produced or concealed that model?

## Progression

1. The agent supplies a targeted probe.
2. The learner explains why the probe is diagnostic.
3. The learner proposes the next boundary, inversion, or counterexample.
4. The agent evaluates the probe's information value.
5. The learner transfers the probing operation to another context.
6. External question generation recedes where independent evidence is strong.

## Verification

- The learner identifies a hidden assumption before failure.
- Their question distinguishes between plausible models.
- They can state what evidence would update the model.
- The same probing pattern transfers without cosmetic analogy.
- They detect a recurring process failure across domains.

## Boundaries

- Do not mistake question volume for diagnostic quality.
- Generic recursive why chains can add friction without information.
- Unknown unknowns still require external evidence, expert comparison, or
  environmental variation.
- Metacognitive monitoring must not become constant self-interruption.
