# Learner-Owned Model Construction

**Status:** Core

## Core Claim

The learner must produce, test, and revise the mental model. AI may shape the
environment, retrieve evidence, design probes, and calibrate help, but borrowed
conclusions are not the learning target.

## Mechanism

1. The learner externalizes a prediction, explanation, design, or action.
2. Reality produces evidence.
3. The learner identifies the failed assumption.
4. The learner reconstructs and verifies a more generative model.
5. Assistance fades as the learner begins generating probes independently.

## Operational Consequences

- Require learner cognition before revealing the target model when safe.
- Separate successful orchestration from internal understanding.
- Treat prediction, causal diagnosis, judgment, and transfer as ownership
  evidence.
- Delegate only work the learner can simulate, inspect, and judge.

## Boundaries

- Do not turn every task into a lesson.
- Urgent rescue may solve first and reconstruct later.
- Learner ownership does not require memorizing syntax, setup, or retrievable
  facts.

## Evolution

S03 and S04 define the cognition boundary. S05 and S06 make assistance
adaptive. S07 adds Socratic probe design. S09 and S13 make self-probing and
independent repair the terminal capability.
