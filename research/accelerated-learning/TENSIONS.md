# Tension Ledger

This file records apparent conflicts across the corpus. A synthesis is accepted
only when it preserves the conditions behind both claims.

## 1. Theory First vs Zero Theory First

**Claims**

- Orient around problem, primitives, types, and non-goals before coding.
- Begin with a concrete exercise and let theory answer discovered confusion.

Sources:
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S11](sources/S11-learning-system-for-trpc.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Use minimum sufficient framing, then immediate contact with an
executable or applied case. Skip framing when the opening task itself can safely
diagnose the prerequisite model. Add framing when the learner cannot form a
meaningful prediction.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 2. AI After Thinking vs AI-Designed Diagnosis

**Claims**

- Asking too early starves model formation.
- The agent should proactively design the first diagnostic probe.

Sources:
[S03](sources/S03-ai-and-cognitive-softness.md),
[S04](sources/S04-cognitive-scaffolding-shift.md),
[S07](sources/S07-learning-with-ai.md),
[S13](sources/S13-threshold-unlocks.md).

**Resolution:** AI may select the situation, retrieve minimum orientation, or
ask the first question before the learner has an answer. It should preserve an
interpretation window: the learner supplies the first hypothesis, prediction,
prioritization, or attempted reconstruction before explanatory closure when
safe.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 3. Failed-Assumption Discovery as Overhead vs Core Work

**Claims**

- The tool should surface which invariant or assumption is involved.
- Identifying the failed assumption is the model-repair act the learner must
  perform.

Sources:
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md),
[S11](sources/S11-learning-system-for-trpc.md),
[S13](sources/S13-threshold-unlocks.md).

**Resolution:** AI may retrieve evidence, contrast prediction with outcome, and
offer candidate hypotheses. The learner should select, justify, or reconstruct
the causal diagnosis before it is recorded as a model change.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 4. Substrates First vs High-Leverage Threshold Early

**Claims**

- Automate low-level models before advanced work.
- Move high-leverage thresholds earlier even when conventional curricula delay
  them.

Sources:
[S01](sources/S01-actual-roadmap.md),
[S02](sources/S02-elite-hacker-roadmap.md).

**Resolution:** Choose the highest-leverage threshold among currently reachable
nodes. Teach only the prerequisites required for a meaningful collision and
verification, not every conventional prerequisite.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 5. Easy-to-Hard vs Leverage-Dependency Order

**Claims**

- Difficulty should be calibrated near current capability.
- Curriculum order should follow future leverage rather than simple difficulty.

Sources:
[S01](sources/S01-actual-roadmap.md),
[S02](sources/S02-elite-hacker-roadmap.md),
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md).

**Resolution:** Leverage chooses the destination; current capability chooses the
size and scaffolding of the next step.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 6. Real Project vs Isolated Toy World

**Claims**

- Real projects provide meaningful stakes and authentic failures.
- New paradigms should be isolated so one variable changes at a time.

Sources:
[S02](sources/S02-elite-hacker-roadmap.md),
[S06](sources/S06-ai-in-dev-workflows.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Begin from the project when possible. Move to a sandbox when
project complexity prevents causal diagnosis, failure is too costly, or a
missing primitive blocks useful action. Return after the model survives a
baseline and boundary prediction and can be reapplied to a concrete project
action. Define the isolated question and return condition before leaving.

See [Operational Heuristics](OPERATIONAL-HEURISTICS.md#project-to-sandbox).

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 7. Session Closure vs Deliberate Open Loops

**Claims**

- End with one clear compressed rule.
- Always leave an unresolved thread to pull the learner back.

Sources:
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md).

**Resolution:** Require conceptual closure for the completed model repair.
Optionally record the next authentic question, but never manufacture
incompleteness or withhold closure to create compulsion.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 8. Short Sessions vs Deep Absorption

**Claims**

- Cap rounds and stop before fatigue.
- Care and flow may sustain long, energizing sessions.

Sources:
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md),
[S12](sources/S12-energy-and-engagement.md).

**Resolution:** Use attention quality, error quality, and recovery signals rather
than a universal duration. Stop or change mode when predictions become careless,
mistakes become noisy, or the learner ceases to repair models.

**Status:** Synthesized; measurement remains open.

## 9. Productive Pressure vs Psychological Safety

**Claims**

- Stakes, challenge, and pressure can sharpen attention.
- Threat, shame, antagonism, and identity pain can corrupt learning.

Sources:
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S12](sources/S12-energy-and-engagement.md).

**Resolution:** Use task-intrinsic stakes, clear constraints, and optional
performance conditions. Reject personal threat, synthetic enemies, humiliation,
and identity manipulation.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 10. No Full Solutions vs Adaptive Delegation

**Claims**

- Preserve struggle and escalate hints gradually.
- Familiar, auditable work should be delegated for leverage.

Sources:
[S05](sources/S05-delayed-growth.md),
[S06](sources/S06-ai-in-dev-workflows.md),
[S07](sources/S07-learning-with-ai.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Assistance depends on mission and demonstrated ownership.
Learning mode preserves target cognition. Delegation mode permits complete
execution when the learner can simulate and review it. Urgent rescue may solve
first and reconstruct later.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 11. Correct Output vs Correct Reasoning

**Claims**

- Real artifacts and working systems are required evidence.
- Correct output from wrong reasoning is a diagnostic failure.

Sources:
[S02](sources/S02-elite-hacker-roadmap.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Track outcome and model quality separately. A working artifact
does not prove transfer; sound reasoning with a calibration error is not a
conceptual failure.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 12. Prerequisite Graph vs Mutable Belief Graph

**Claims**

- Concepts have prerequisite dependencies.
- Learners carry idiosyncratic, context-dependent misconceptions discovered
  only through responses.

Sources:
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S13](sources/S13-threshold-unlocks.md).

**Resolution:** Maintain separate but linked structures:

- A domain model of prerequisites, thresholds, and verification criteria
- A learner evidence model of beliefs, confidence, contexts, and transitions

Neither graph should be treated as a perfect fixed DAG.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 13. Build Until Blocked vs Proactive Curriculum

**Claims**

- Switch from shipping to learning only when a concrete blocker appears.
- Proactively expose unknown unknowns and sequence threshold experiences.

Sources:
[S07](sources/S07-learning-with-ai.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Mission determines cadence:

- Development mode uses blockers and risk signals to trigger the shortest
  focused path back to artifact progress.
- Research mode proactively traverses and revises a compounding threshold graph.
- Development may schedule brief consolidation or adversarial review without
  expanding into unbounded research.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 14. Mechanical Questions vs Conceptual Priority

**Claims**

- Redirect mechanical questions toward principles.
- The learner chooses which friction they intend to face.

Sources:
[S03](sources/S03-ai-and-cognitive-softness.md),
[S10](sources/S10-forking-and-cloning-workflow.md),
[S11](sources/S11-learning-system-for-trpc.md).

**Resolution:** Answer mechanical retrieval directly when it is non-target
friction. Escalate to concepts only when the mechanic reveals or blocks the
declared target model.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 15. Every Stage as Threshold vs Supporting Fluency

**Claims**

- Curriculum stages should be transformations.
- Some knowledge and maintenance practice do not produce a perceptual shift.

Sources:
[S01](sources/S01-actual-roadmap.md),
[S02](sources/S02-elite-hacker-roadmap.md).

**Resolution:** Thresholds organize modules and gates. Supporting retrieval,
fluency, and prerequisite work lives inside or between them without being
misrepresented as a threshold.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 16. Recency vs Causal Completeness

**Claims**

- Later transcripts are often more refined.
- Later passages sometimes escalate rhetoric without improving the mechanism.

Sources:
[METHOD](METHOD.md),
[S08](sources/S08-vscode-extension-guide.md),
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md).

**Resolution:** Prefer later claims only when they add a condition, boundary,
sequence, failure mode, or stronger evidence. Do not prefer them merely because
they are later or more intense.

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 17. Avoidance Detection vs Legitimate Detour

**Claims**

- Planning, research, and refactoring can become escape paths.
- Scope correction, rest, prerequisite repair, and environment preparation can
  be necessary.

Sources:
[S14](sources/S14-thdxr-post.md),
[S15](sources/S15-matt-pocock-post.md),
[S16](sources/S16-friction-avoidance-hell.md).

**Resolution:** Do not infer avoidance from activity type alone. Require a
pattern: repeated departure from the declared target without new evidence,
artifact progress, a model update, or an explicit return condition.

Test a detour by asking which uncertainty it resolves, what evidence ends it,
and what target action resumes. Record behavior rather than a psychological
label.

See [Operational Heuristics](OPERATIONAL-HEURISTICS.md#avoidance-vs-legitimate-detour).

**Status:** Synthesized.

## 18. Persistent Context vs Privacy and Staleness

**Claims**

- Global learner state enables analogies and recursive why calibration.
- Local project state enables grounded, version-specific exercises.

Sources:
[S09](sources/S09-socrates-product-exploration.md),
[S11](sources/S11-learning-system-for-trpc.md).

**Resolution**

- Treat stored state as evidence-backed claims with provenance and scope.
- Revalidate on context change, contradiction, or high-leverage reuse; age
  alone is insufficient.
- Mark contradicted evidence `challenged` and link replacements as
  `superseded`; do not silently overwrite history.
- Keep learner-wide evidence under `~/.learning/` so verified anchors and
  patterns can cross workspaces.
- Keep mission, version, artifact, and project findings under the current
  workspace's `.learning/`.
- Promote local evidence globally only after compression and transfer or
  reconstruction evidence.
- Sensitive motivational data requires opt-in.

See [State Contracts](STATE-CONTRACTS.md#evidence-lifecycle).

**Status:** Synthesized and approved.
